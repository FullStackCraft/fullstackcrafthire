This Independent Contractor Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of [Start Date], by and between:

Company: Full Stack Craft LLC (operated by Christopher Frewin)
Website: https://fullstackcraft.com

(“Company”)

and

Contractor: [Full Legal Name], residing in [Country]
(“Contractor”)

1. Nature of Engagement

The Company engages the Contractor as an independent contractor, not as an employee, partner, or agent. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship, partnership, or joint venture.

This Agreement is for an indefinite period of time, commencing on the Start Date, unless terminated in accordance with Section 10.

2. Scope of Work

The Contractor shall provide full-stack software development services, including but not limited to:

Building and maintaining features across fintech and developer tooling products

Backend development primarily using Golang

Frontend development using TypeScript, React, and Next.js

Working with databases and services such as PostgreSQL (Supabase), Stripe, and serverless infrastructure

Participating in technical discussions, architecture decisions, and product direction

Iterative development with rapid feedback from real users

The Contractor may also, by mutual agreement, take on mentoring or leadership responsibilities as the Company scales.

3. Time Commitment & Availability

3.1 Baseline Commitment

The expected workload is approximately 50% full-time equivalent, averaging ~20 hours per week.

3.2 Crunch Periods

From time to time, the Company may request increased availability during defined “crunch periods,” up to 60–80% full-time equivalent.

Crunch periods shall:

Be temporary

Be communicated in advance where reasonably possible

Never exceed two (2) consecutive weeks, unless mutually agreed with additional compensation or time-off arrangements

3.3 Workload Balancing

To ensure fairness:

Increased workload during crunch periods will be balanced by lighter weeks afterward, or

Compensated through mutually agreed additional pay or time-off adjustments

The intent is that the average workload over a monthly period remains approximately 50%.

4. Compensation
4.1 Base Compensation

The Company shall pay the Contractor $500 USD per month, equivalent to $6,000 USD per year, for the baseline 50% commitment.

Payments shall be made on the 15th and 30th of each month via any method of the Company's and Contractor’s mutual choosing (e.g., PayPal, TransferWise, bank transfer).

4.2 Reviews & Increases

Compensation will be reviewed every three months based on:

Product growth

Revenue performance

Scope of responsibility

Contractor’s contribution and ownership mindset

This compensation represents a starting point, not a ceiling.

4.3 Revenue Sharing (Future)

The parties acknowledge an intent to explore revenue-sharing or performance-based compensation for the right long-term partnership. Any such arrangement shall be documented in writing as an amendment to this Agreement.

5. Ownership & Intellectual Property

All work products, including but not limited to source code, designs, documentation, and inventions created by the Contractor in the course of this engagement (“Work Product”), shall be considered work made for hire and shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, the Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns all rights, title, and interest to the Company.

6. Confidentiality

The Contractor agrees to keep confidential all non-public information related to the Company, its products, customers, financials, and business strategy.

This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

7. Autonomy & Tools

The Contractor retains discretion over how the work is performed.

The Contractor shall provide their own equipment, tools, and workspace.

Work may be performed remotely from the Contractor’s location.

8. Non-Exclusivity

This Agreement is non-exclusive. The Contractor may engage in other work, provided it does not:

Compete directly with the Company’s products, or

Interfere materially with obligations under this Agreement

9. Taxes

The Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes, duties, and statutory obligations arising from payments made under this Agreement. The Company will not withhold taxes.

10. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause by providing 30 days written notice.

The Company may terminate immediately for:

Material breach of this Agreement

Gross misconduct

Violation of confidentiality or IP obligations

Upon termination, the Contractor shall be paid for all work performed up to the effective termination date.

11. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [choose: Switzerland / England & Wales / Contractor’s country — pick one intentionally], without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

12. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions or representations. Any amendments must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
