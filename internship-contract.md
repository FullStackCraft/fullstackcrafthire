Internship Agreement

(35 Hours Per Week)

This Internship Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of February 1st, 2026, by and between:

Company: Full Stack Craft LLC (operated by Christopher Frewin)
Website: https://fullstackcraft.com

("Company")

and

Intern: [Full Legal Name], residing in [Country]
("Intern")

1. Nature of Engagement

The Company engages the Intern to perform internship duties. This Agreement is intended to set out the expectations and terms of the internship. Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the Intern is not an employee for purposes of benefits or employment law under this Agreement.

This Agreement is for an indefinite period, commencing on February 1st, 2026, unless terminated in accordance with Section 10.

2. Scope of Work

The Intern shall provide software development and related support services, including but not limited to:

- Assisting in building and maintaining features across fintech and developer tooling products
- Backend support primarily using Golang
- Frontend work using TypeScript, React, and Next.js
- Working with databases and services such as PostgreSQL (Supabase), Stripe, and serverless infrastructure
- Participating in technical discussions and learning through product-focused work

By mutual agreement the Intern may take on mentoring tasks, documentation, or other responsibilities appropriate to their skills and growth.

3. Time Commitment & Availability

3.1 Baseline Commitment

The expected workload is approximately 35 hours per week (approximately 87.5% full-time (40 hour week) equivalent).

3.2 Crunch Periods

From time to time, the Company may request increased availability during defined “crunch periods,” up to 100% full-time equivalent for short durations.

Crunch periods shall:

- Be temporary
- Be communicated in advance where reasonably possible
- Never exceed two (2) consecutive weeks, unless mutually agreed with additional compensation or time-off arrangements

3.3 Workload Balancing

To ensure fairness:

- Increased workload during crunch periods will be balanced by lighter weeks afterward, or
- Compensated through mutually agreed additional pay or time-off adjustments

The intent is that the average workload over a monthly period remains consistent with the baseline commitment.

4. Compensation
4.1 Base Compensation

The Company shall pay the Intern $875 USD per month, equivalent to $10,500 USD per year, for the baseline 35-hour weekly commitment for services rendered.

Payments shall be made once per month on the last day of each month via any method of the Company's and Intern’s mutual choosing (e.g., PayPal, Wise, bank transfer).

4.2 Reviews & Increases

Compensation will be reviewed every three months based on:

- Product growth
- Revenue performance
- Scope of responsibility
- Intern’s contribution and learning progress

This compensation represents a starting point and may be revised as responsibilities or commitment change.

4.3 Future Performance Arrangements

The parties may explore performance-based compensation or other incentives as the relationship evolves. Any such arrangement shall be documented as an amendment to this Agreement.

5. Ownership & Intellectual Property

All work products, including but not limited to source code, designs, documentation, and inventions created by the Intern in the course of this engagement ("Work Product"), shall be considered work made for hire and shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work made for hire, the Intern hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest to the Company.

6. Confidentiality

The Intern agrees to keep confidential all non-public information related to the Company, its products, customers, financials, and business strategy. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement.

7. Autonomy & Tools

The Intern will receive guidance and goals from the Company but will have discretion over how to accomplish assigned work. The Intern shall provide their own equipment, tools, and workspace unless otherwise agreed.

Work may be performed remotely from the Intern’s location.

8. Non-Exclusivity

This Agreement is non-exclusive. The Intern may engage in other work or coursework, provided it does not:

- Compete directly with the Company’s products, or
- Interfere materially with obligations under this Agreement

9. Taxes

The Intern is solely responsible for all taxes, duties, and statutory obligations arising from payments made under this Agreement. The Company will not withhold taxes unless required by applicable law.

10. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause by providing at least 30 days' (one month) written notice. Both parties must provide at least one month's written notice before quitting or resigning.

The Company may terminate immediately for:

- Material breach of this Agreement
- Gross misconduct
- Violation of confidentiality or IP obligations

Upon termination, the Intern shall be paid for all work performed up to the effective termination date.

11. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [choose: Switzerland / England & Wales / Intern’s country — pick one intentionally], without regard to conflict-of-law principles.

12. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions or representations. Any amendments must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
